# InsP6 Translation Boundary

## Purpose

This document prevents overclaiming around phytic acid/InsP6 and plant-source foods while preserving the mechanistic rationale for the barrier-axis study.

## Mechanistic Anchor

The source paper is "Phytic acid (InsP6) activates HDAC3 epigenetic axis to maintain intestinal barrier function" in Nature Communications, DOI `10.1038/s41467-026-68994-0`.

## Permitted Language

- "The InsP6-HDAC3 study provides mechanistic rationale for examining the IPMK-HDAC3-MMP barrier axis in human IBD cohorts."
- "Phytic acid/InsP6 is a plant-derived molecule enriched in plant-based foods such as rice and legumes, but the present study does not test diet or supplementation."
- "Future prospective nutrition or intervention studies would be needed to evaluate whether InsP6 modifies IBD outcomes in humans."
- "The present analysis tests the human clinical relevance of a barrier-axis state, not the efficacy of InsP6."

## Forbidden Language

- "Phytic acid treats IBD."
- "Rice, legumes, or high-phytate diets improve mucosal healing."
- "InsP6 supplementation is clinically validated for IBD."
- "Our public-data analysis proves dietary intervention efficacy."
- "Patients should increase phytate intake based on this study."

## Plant-Source Wording

Use "plant-source foods that can contain phytic acid/InsP6" only as background context. Do not nominate a single plant as the intervention unless a future study has direct dietary exposure data.

## Human Evidence Boundary

The current project can test whether the IPMK-HDAC3-MMP barrier-axis state is associated with clinical endpoints in human IBD cohorts. It cannot test whether exogenous InsP6 changes that axis in patients.

## Manuscript Placement

- Introduction: one short mechanistic rationale paragraph.
- Discussion: one bounded translational paragraph.
- Abstract: avoid InsP6 treatment language unless explicitly framed as rationale.
